Frequently asked questions about Ottawa rent

What is the average rent in Ottawa in 2025?

The average rent for a 1-bedroom apartment in Ottawa is about $2,050 per month in 2025, based on CMHC data. A 2-bedroom averages about $2,650 per month. Rents vary significantly by neighbourhood.

What is the average rent in Vanier, Ottawa?

Vanier is one of Ottawa's most affordable central neighbourhoods. Average rents run about 15% below the Ottawa average, making it one of the best value areas close to the downtown core.

What is the average rent in the Glebe, Ottawa?

The Glebe is one of Ottawa's most sought-after neighbourhoods. Rents typically run about 20% above the Ottawa average, reflecting high demand and proximity to the Rideau Canal and Bank Street.

What is the average rent in Westboro, Ottawa?

Westboro rents typically run about 18% above the Ottawa average. It is a high-demand neighbourhood in the west end, known for its shops and proximity to the Ottawa River and LRT.

Is rent controlled in Ottawa?

Yes, for units first occupied before November 15, 2018. The Ontario annual rent increase guideline for 2026 is 2.1%. Units first occupied after November 2018 are not rent controlled between tenancies.

How do I check if my Ottawa rent is fair?

Use the Ottawa Rent Calculator on this page. Enter your neighbourhood, unit type, and monthly rent. We compare it to CMHC data and anonymous Ottawa renter submissions to show you a fair range and a confidence score.

What is a fair rent in Ottawa for a bachelor apartment?

A fair rent for a bachelor or studio apartment in Ottawa is roughly $1,400 to $1,850 per month in 2025, depending on the neighbourhood. The median is about $1,600 per month.
